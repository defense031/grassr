## The ratified card convention (v2)

For each agreement coefficient at the matched design (F/pi-hat, k, N),
evaluate the observed value against EVERY calibrated q cell (no cell
selection, no q snapping):

1. **Sweep profile**: P_q = CDF_q(q_hat_obs) for each calibrated q —
   where the observation falls within the sampling distribution a
   panel of quality q would produce at this design. The card's
   graphic (plot_position(): ridgeline of the q cells, observed value
   as a line, consistent region shaded — sibling of
   delta_null_distributions).
2. **Consistency band** (the quality statement — test inversion):
   qualities q with 0.025 <= P_q <= 0.975 are consistent with the
   data; band endpoints interpolated linearly in q where P_q crosses
   0.975 (lower end) and 0.025 (upper end). Band level 95%
   (two-sided, alpha = 0.05). If P_q is non-monotone in q from MC
   noise, take the OUTERMOST crossings (conservative wide band).
   Open-ended bands at the grid boundary (q = 0.55 or 0.99) are
   reported as open ("<= 0.57" / ">= 0.97") with a boundary flag.
3. **Pooled percentile** (the categorical reading): trapezoid-weighted
   average of P_q over the q axis [0.55, 0.99] (trapezoid because the
   grid is non-uniform: 0.05 steps to 0.90, 0.02 after). Reads as
   "position within the design's achievable agreement range."
   Monotone in the observed coefficient by construction.

Card line (target): "PABAK 0.62 — 55th percentile of this design's
achievable range; consistent with panel quality 0.89-0.91."

## The delta-hat fork (AWAITING Austin — gates the compute plan)

delta-hat is currently the max-min spread of the family coefficients'
surface percentiles — computed through the RETIRED sawtooth
machinery. The 24.85M-draw null (stage 6) and the 11M-draw power
program (stage 7) stored ONLY per-draw delta values (storage audit
2026-07-05), so they cannot be converted arithmetically. Tier-2 arms
stored raw coefficients — arithmetic recompute under any option.

- **Option A — spread of pooled percentiles.** delta-hat = max-min of
  the family's pooled percentiles. Smooth, no sawtooth. Requires
  stage 6 + stage 7 regeneration.
- **Option B — spread of implied qualities (RECOMMENDED).** delta-hat
  = max-min of the family's inverted q-hats, in quality units. Each
  coefficient implies a panel quality; if the model held exactly they
  would agree; the spread is model discordance in interpretable pp of
  quality ("the three coefficients imply qualities 0.84/0.86/0.91 — a
  7pp spread, 98th percentile of chance at your design"). No
  percentile machinery inside delta-hat at all; cheapest per-draw
  regeneration; aligns with the band-on-quality card; the flag
  convention (percentile on matched (k, N, q-hat) null, >=95 caution
  / >=99 divergent) is UNCHANGED in form. Requires stage 6 + stage 7
  regeneration. Hypothesis to check on regen: the old null's heavy
  tails / small-N mixture were partly sawtooth amplification; B's
  null should be cleaner and power higher (old TPR 0.38-0.45).
- **Option C — keep legacy delta-hat as-is.** The old percentile
  spread survives as a hidden internal statistic with its
  already-calibrated null (internally consistent: null was generated
  through the same noisy pipeline). Zero re-compute. Cost: two
  percentile conventions coexist in one package (one shipped, one
  hidden inside delta-hat); delta-hat keeps sawtooth-inflated noise
  (lower power); the no-voice boundary and null pathologies may be
  partly artifact and would ship uncorrected.
